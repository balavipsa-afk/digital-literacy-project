# Stay Safe Online – Simple Checklist

These are some basic things I think every college student should follow to stay safe online:

- Use strong passwords (not something like 123456 or your name). Try mixing letters, numbers, and symbols.  
- Don’t use the same password everywhere. If one account gets hacked, others can be at risk too.  
- Turn on 2-factor authentication (OTP/login approval) for important apps like Gmail, Instagram, banking apps, etc.  
- Don’t click on random links sent on WhatsApp, Telegram, or email, especially if they look suspicious or too good to be true.  
- Always double-check before sharing any personal info online, even if the person sounds genuine.  

**UPI / Payment Safety:**
- Never share your UPI PIN or OTP with anyone. No bank or official person will ever ask for it.  
- Don’t scan unknown QR codes or accept random “request money” notifications on UPI apps.  

- Try not to post too much personal info publicly (like phone number, address, college ID, etc.).  
- Keep your apps and phone updated so security issues are fixed regularly.  
- If something feels off, trust your instinct and avoid it. It’s better to be safe than sorry.  

**If something goes wrong, report it immediately:**
- National Cyber Crime Portal: https://www.cybercrime.gov.in  
- Helpline Number: 1930  

> These are simple habits, but they can actually prevent a lot of problems if followed properly.
